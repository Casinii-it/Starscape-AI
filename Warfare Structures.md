Warfare Structures

*This page focuses on the defensive and industrial aspects of contested systems. Check the Faction Warfare page for more information on the core mechanics and offensive gameplay.*

Warfare structures are a set of defense and industrial modules designed to prevent, delay, or assist players in defending against incursions in contested systems, as well as provide services that aren't always available in these systems.

## Resupplying

![](./File:HQCargoDock.png)

Headquarters cargo dock in Edgroo, Valence.

Supplies are cargo items acquired in supply docks of dockyard station modules located in the staging areas of the warring factions, as well as Harbor and Syndica. They can be hauled to the system headquarters cargo dock or to constructors scattered throughout the system. Trade Union and Syndicate supplies cannot be delivered to constructors.

## System Headquarters

![](./File:ForkHQStronghold.png)

Foralkan stronghold headquarters station.

The HQ is a military station protected by an invulnerable shield that allows passage only to players enlisted to its faction. Delivering sufficient supplies to this station unlocks constructors for defense and industrial structures, as well as new modules for the station.

### Features

This station has unique characteristics that set it apart from other station types. In the main corridor, there are 3 signs and 2 maps.

**- Warfare Region Status** shows how many systems each faction has in this sector, also showing their defense tier.

**- System Status** shows the progress towards the next defense tier and some of its benefits.

**- Status of Structures** shows how many structures are built or can be built.

**- The Region Map** shows a map that only highlights the sector the system is located in, and to which faction each system belongs.

**- The Tactical Map** shows a system map that highlights allied and enemy ships and structures.

## Primary Defense Structures

**- System HQs,** beyond having the services provided by its station modules, also have defensive value for its docking bay turrets and HQ fleet, composed of a cruiser, destroyers, frigates, fighters, and interceptors.

**- Generators** are responsible for maintaining the shield around the system HQ. Upgrading the system HQ will also improve the generator's defense and offense. All 3 generators in a system must be disabled to attack the Sovereignty Beacon and capture the system.

**- Turret Towers** are located next to the HQ, boasting 2L and 8M turrets each, a much higher range, and the ability to notify all allied NPC ships nearby of the target's location.

## Secondary Defense Structures

Constructors require a certain amount of supplies to begin construction. They are easily located by being near a neon construction site. Supplying secondary defense structures does not reward credits or faction standing.

**- Fleet Hangars** spawn NPC fighters (+6 per tier) that patrol points of interest in the system, responding to the presence of a enemy player at a warp hub. The generated ships are gradually replaced if destroyed.

**- Defense Platforms** project a shield over the structure they are defending, requiring all platforms to be destroyed to expose the structure to direct damage. Constructors are found around generators and fleet hangars

**- Capital Shipyards** construct Homeland Battleships, always located on the same planet as the HQ. After the shipyard is constructed or the battleship is destroyed, it takes 1 hour for another battleship to be generated. Once the battleship's construction is complete, it will emit a loud foghorn siren, audible to any player in the warp hub. It will then patrol the area around the shipyard, attacking any hostile players or NPCs.

**- Mining Platforms** improve the quality of ores generated in the planetary asteroid field around them and protect it from rogue drones and pirates.
