Vesta

*“The Vesta is a large combined miner-hauler. While not as capable of a miner as the Kodiak, its cargo capacity allows it to sustain unaided mining expeditions for longer periods of time.”*

The Vesta is a miner-class ship of the Koronis Line. It is best suited as a powerful long-distance mining warship.

## Advantages

The Vesta features 8 cargo slots, keeping in line with the Mining Guild line's focus on space. It can take longer trips into wild space, and is effectively a direct upgrade to the Eos. Were a Vesta to fill all its crates to the brim, it would outclass even the Yukon in terms of profit from a single run (50,000 refined common ores compared to the Yukon's 20,000). The Vesta also has a fair amount of PDT slots, along with a weapon rig, lending it more defensive capabilities compared to other warship miners that focus on industrial efficiency.

## Disadvantages

As a miner, it is less agile than other ships of its class. Due to its many cargo crates, it is a juicy target, and it does not have much in the way of combat capabilities: it has less hitpoints compared to other frigates, with rigs mostly focused on industry. Compared to the Kodiak, it has sacrificed mining efficiency for longer mining periods.

As a faction ship, its blueprint must be purchased using Loyalty Points at a faction terminal or from other players, making it less accessible to new or neutral players.

## Trivia

* The current Vesta is modeled by swindle150 and ii\_Cove.
* The Vesta is most likely named after 4 Vesta, the second-largest protoplanet (to Ceres) in the asteroid belt. This is also supported by the fact that the shapes of the protoplanet and the ship are irregular.
* Prior to the *Getting Around* update, this was a Trade Union ship.
