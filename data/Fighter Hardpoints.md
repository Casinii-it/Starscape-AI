Fighter Hardpoints

![](./File:FTurret.png)

Stratos' F turret selection menu.

Fighter hardpoints, spinal guns, or also referred to in-game as F turrets, are fixed hardpoints similar to P turrets. Players can switch them in the ship editing menu by selecting the hardpoint and then the preferred one for a credit fee.

Fighter hardpoints are forward-facing turrets carried by all non-hauler starships, with fighters and interceptors carrying combat hardpoint (Burst, Chaingun, and Pulse) and small miners carrying industrial hardpoints (Detonator and Mining Beam).

## Hardpoint Information

| Hardpoint | Description | Observations |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Burst | *"A forward-facing laser cannon which is considered standard issue on most ships."* | Burst is the default F turret and the most consistent damage dealer. |
| Chaingun | *"A forward-facing chaingun which delivers kinetic damage to its targets."* | Chaingun's kinetic multiplier is very effective when targeting already hulled player ships in fleet battles. |
| Pulse | *"A forward-facing pulse laser designed to deliver deadly accurate damage to targets."* | Pulse fires near hitscan shots, great for cheesing NPC enemies from a range, though not as effective in close-range engagements. |
| Detonator | *"An industrial hardpoint which can be used to blast off several chunks of ore at a time from a target asteroid."* | Detonator is the most efficient industrial hardpoint to actively mine with. The detonator's rate of fire is affected by Rapid Fire rigs, and each projectile has a fixed average release rate of 3. |
| Mining Beam | *"An industrial hardpoint which fires a concentrated mining beam at asteroids to retrieve ores."* | Beams function identically to miner turrets. They are generally useful for AFK mining in the early-game for their 65% capture rate. |
