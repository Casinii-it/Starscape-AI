Missions are repeatable quests that factions can give to the player, providing rewards of credits, skill points, and standing with that particular faction.

## Mechanics

To accept a mission from a faction, the player must first find a field office station module (it is recommended to use the Agent Finder tool on the sidebar of the galaxy map by pressing the M key), located at non-neutral stations and military stations in their permanent territory, and speak to the agents inside. The agent's level must be equal to or lower than the player's standing tier with the faction in question; otherwise, the agent will not give missions. Higher-level agents may assign missions one tier below theirs.

Missions usually require the player to travel to one or more mission sites in other systems, then return to the field office once completed, and speak to the agent to receive the rewards.

### Reward Modifiers

* Missions from Trade Union and Mining Guild agents reward 25% more credits; missions from the Syndicate reward 50% more credits.
* Negotiation and Diplomacy skills increase, respectively, the credit and standing rewards from missions by 5% per level.
* Destroy: [Enemy Faction] missions reward twice the credits and standing compared to other missions.

### Intel Recovery

Collect intel from Covert Ops ships (NPC fighters) in multiple systems; 5 different scenarios will randomize the mission:

* The Covert Ops ship is ready to give the intel as the player arrives (always the first scenario). This will be on every mission.
* The Covert Ops ship is destroyed, and the player needs to find the [Faction] black box.
  + There's a chance that pirates warp in to fight the player after finding the black box.
* The Covert Ops ship is under attack; it has the same difficulty as the 3rd scenario.
* The Covert Ops ship's pilot thinks they were betrayed, attacks the player, and the player has to kill them.

### Office Supply Run

Equip a hauler-ship, travel to an outpost in another system, pick up supplies (mission cargo), and deliver them to the cargo dock of the station where the player accepted this mission.

* T0: 2 cargo supplies.
* T2: 6 cargo supplies.
* T4: 10 cargo supplies.

### Resupply the Outpost

Equip a hauler-ship, pick up supplies (mission cargo) at the cargo dock of the station where the player accepted this mission, and deliver them to an outpost in another system.

* T0: 2 cargo supplies.
* T2: 6 cargo supplies.
* T4: 10 cargo supplies.

### Salvage Operation

Equip a hauler-ship, travel to a mission site with a battlefield surrounded by a minefield, salvage all cargo containers, and take them to the cargo dock of the station where the player accepted this mission.

* T1: 2 cargo containers.
* T3: 5 cargo containers.
* T5: 8 cargo containers.

### Law Enforcement

Travel to a mission site with a regular korrelite asteroid field, drive 3 pirate miners away by destroying at least one, and reactivate the proximity alarms while fending off occasional waves of pirates after activating each alarm.

* T0: 2 fighters and 2 interceptors.
* T2: 2 fighters, 2 interceptors, and 1 corvette.
* T4: 2 fighters, 2 interceptors, and 2 corvettes.

### Destroy: [Enemy Faction]

These missions require the player to destroy a specific number of ships from a faction to complete the mission.

* T1: destroy 20 fighters **or** destroy 10 fighters and 3 corvettes.
* T3: destroy 10 fighters, 10 interceptors, and 10 corvettes, **or** 10 corvettes and 3 frigates.
* T5: destroy 10 fighters, 10 interceptors, 10 corvettes, and 3 frigates **or** 10 frigates and 3 destroyers.

## Trivia

* The enemy ships and structures encountered in the *Defend the Transport*, *Attack the Base*, *Rescue Operation*, and *Destroy: [Enemy Faction*] missions vary depending on the relationship of the faction the player accepted the mission from with the others.
  + Missions from Kavani agents have Lycentian and Foralkan enemies; Lycentian agents, Kavani and Foralkan enemies; Foralkan agents, Kavani and Lycentian enemies; CoreSec, drone enemies; Union and Guild agents, pirate enemies; Syndicate agents, drone and pirate enemies.
* Transport ships have approximately 250k hull and, unless the scenario where the pilot attacks the player plays out, Covert Ops ships have approximately 100k shield.
  + Destroying those ships will cause the player to lose 10 standing with the faction the ship is associated with.
