Death

![](./File:PlayerTagIcon.png)

Player tag icon.

In a semi-hardcore game like Starscape, players should expect to “die” against their will at some point. However, there is no true death, as players will still respawn in a cloning bay and live on.

## Death Consequences

Dying, regardless of the scenario, has some standard consequences: inventory items, weapons, and armor are lost. Dying on a ship has the additional effects of losing all ship modifications (or most of them, depending on the level of the Reclaimer and Elite Reclaimer skills) and the ship itself until it is fully salvaged or the player pays for the recovery fee in a repair bay station module.

Things change in wild space because getting destroyed on a ship has the added effect of losing it permanently. Note that if a player dies from ground combat in a frontier outpost, their ship isn't lost.

### Player Tags

Tags are decorative items collected from the wreckage or bodies of dead players, marked with their username. They can also be placed inside display cases in habitats or traded.

## Cloning

When players die in Starscape, they respawn at the cloning bay set beforehand, which is the Citadel by default, and will have to pay a fee of 250 credits if they died in a PvP-disabled system or a 500 fee if they died in a PvP-enabled system. Players won't be charged if they don't have enough credits.

Having a tier III reputation with an NPC faction gives players the "cloning rights" to respawn at their capital system. To set your spawn location, find the faction's cloning bay and set your spawn with 1000 credits at the terminal at the far end of the room.

### Resetting

The option to reset your character is always available; it is useful to players who don't want to waste time jumping back to systems with higher player activity. Before resetting, a message appears asking the player to confirm they want to proceed.

Note that if you are going to reset, store all your items, unequip weapons and armor, and do it inside a station to avoid losing a ship (or do this in a Stratos, as you prefer).
