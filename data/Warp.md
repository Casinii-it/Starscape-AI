Warp

![](./File:Halo_in_Warp.png)

Halo inside a warp field.

Waiting for your ship to travel between spaceboxes is how players are going to spend a considerable amount of their playtime. Several variants of travel ultimately are funneled down to region travel - between spaceboxes in the same server - and interstellar travel - between spaceboxes in different servers.

When selecting a destination to travel, the ship will automatically reduce its speed until it stops and begin aligning itself with the selected destination. The direction the ship was previously pointing, as well as its acceleration and agility stats, determine how long it takes to align itself.

After the ship is aligned and stationary, it will start a universal 3-second jumping wind-up. The travel speed will increase until it is approximately halfway to its destination, at which point it will begin to decelerate until it reaches its destination.

Aligning, jumping, and charging can be cancelled at any time by pressing X, up until the ship begins traveling.

## Travel Methods

### Warp Jump

Warp jumping is the main method of travel, allowing players to travel to warp hubs, anomalies, mission sites, and other star systems.

If the destination is another star system, the engines charge first, and then the jumping wind-up begins. The ship will enter a warp field and keep speeding up until ROBLOX teleports the player to the next server. In the destination system, the player arrives at the warp hub of the planet closest to the origin system, which changes with the planet's orbital periods.

There are three ways to initiate a warp jump:

* Aim at your destination's warp icon with the cursor and press F.
* Hold SPACE to open the quick-warp menu, then move your mouse over your desired destination, then release SPACE to select.
* Open the system map with N or the galaxy map with M, select your desired destination, and click "Warp".

### Lightcruise

![](./File:Radix-Lightcruise.PNG)

Radix lightcruising.

Lightcruising is used to move between subregions of a single planet (i.e., warp hubs, rings, and aberrations). Players can lightcruise through the N key map or the quick-warp menu. Unlike warp jumping, aligning to lightcruise does not point directly at the destination; instead, it angles the ship into orbit so that it can arc to the destination around the planet.

### Energy Requirements

| Variants | Energy required |
| --- | --- |
| Wormhole | 0% |
| Relay Jump |
| Lightcruise | 10% |
| Warp Jump (Region) | 20% |
| Warp Jump (Interstellar) | 40%\* |

(\*) When entering a system after an interstellar warp jump, ships regain about 30% of their energy capacity to compensate for the jump. This means travel will never take longer due to energy costs.

## Warp Disruption

Warp disruptors generate an energy field, preventing any ships affected from generating a warp field. Upon attempting to warp, an error message will appear, and the warp process will not initiate. The words “WARP DISRUPTED” will appear above the hitpoint bar when affected by warp disruption.

Disruption does not affect lightcruising, wormhole traveling, or the use of lightburner or blink drive subsystems.

*Main articles: Warp Disruptor/Disruptor Subsystem.*

## Warp Logging

*“Warp logging”* is a term given to the act of logging out during interstellar travel. It is a way to escape other players, as leaving the game before entering the next system prevents the player's ship from being damaged in the previous system and it from spawning in the next system. Upon rejoining the game, the warp will resume.

Logging during a region warp jump or lightcruise won't make your ship despawn; the ship will remain in the system, susceptible to damage from players and NPCs.

## Known Issues

* ROBLOX error 773 ("Teleport Failed"), 277, or 279 ("Disconnected") may happen during interstellar travel.
* Because of lag on your end:
  + Starscape's anti-cheat might kick you if a lag spike happens while traveling.
  + Your ship can take damage while in warp if you were being shot at when you warped away.

## Trivia

* Ships can warp through planetary objects, other ships, planets, and stars.
* Ships cannot travel while inside a station's docking bay.
* By default, before traveling to a contested, unsecure, or wild system, a prompt will appear warning the player that they are about to go to a PvP-enabled system. This prompt can be disabled in the Settings.
* NPC battleships are the only ship class unable to travel.
* The game states that the LOSSI drive is responsible for enabling ships to lightcruise. According to ZolarKeth[[1]](./Warp#cite_note-1), LOSSI stands for Lightcruise Overdrive Supercruise Sublight Impulse. Additionally, the drive inventor's last name is Lossi.
