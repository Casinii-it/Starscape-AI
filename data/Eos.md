Eos

*"The Eos is a combined miner-hauler developed by the Trade Union. It is primarily intended for long-term unsupported mining expeditions."*

The Eos is a miner-class ship of the Koronis line. It is best suited as a cheap but capable long-distance mining warship.

## Advantages

The Eos features 4 cargo slots, keeping in line with the Mining Guild line's focus on space. As such, it can take longer trips into wild space before requiring a station to store its loot. As with most mining warships, it has a role bonus which makes it more efficient at mining than fighter-style miners. Due to its cargo capacity combined with its relatively low cost, the Eos is a powerful miner when it comes to wild space expeditions.

## Disadvantages

As a miner, it is less agile than other ships of its class. Its cargo crates make it a juicy target for player pirates hoping for a large loot haul, and it does not boast much in the way of combat capabilities - it has less hitpoints compared to other corvettes and rigs mostly focused on industry, not combat. It also does not have any designated combat or self-defense slots, and most of its turrets are focused on maximum mining power.

As a faction ship, its blueprint must be purchased using Loyalty Points at a faction terminal or from other players, making it less accessible to new or neutral players.

## Trivia

* The current Eos is modeled by swindle150 and ii\_Cove.
* Prior to the Getting Around update, this was a Trade Union ship. Its description still claims it was developed by the Trade Union.
