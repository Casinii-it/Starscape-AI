Subsystems

Subsystem is one of the two types of modules in the game, used to enhance ships with special abilities or temporary passives at the cost of energy. They can be equipped in the editing menu in the orange subsystem slots.

## Subsystem Types

Most subsystems can be equipped on any ship. However, the 4 main types differ primarily in their activation energy and energy per second, limiting which ship classes can use them effectively. For example, the Claymore has 80 energy capacity; it can use light and medium hull repairers, but cannot use heavy hull repairers because the required activation energy exceeds what this ship can hold.

Subsystems are usually labeled as light (S for small), medium (M), heavy (L for large), or unlabeled; each label indicates the ship size for which the subsystem operates most efficiently when equipped. Unlabeled subsystems have fixed stats that are compatible with all ships.

Some subsystems are exclusive to ships of specific sizes, such as blink drives, siege modules, and the different types of lightburners.

### Afterburner and Overdrive

Both subsystems temporarily increase the speed stat of the ship at the cost of energy and an agility debuff. Afterburners are exclusive to small ships, while overdrives have M and L versions.

Afterburners are activated by holding the shift key, which automatically accelerates the ship.

Tier X afterburners and overdrives are ancient.

### Hull Booster

Hull boosters exchange energy reserves for hull points. They have 5 levels, and each consumes 15% of the total energy capacity. By pressing the activation key, the booster will already be at level 1 and will charge while holding the key, reaching level 5 after about 1 second.

Tier X hull boosters are ancient.

### Lightburner

Lightburners allow ships to travel up to 6 km (depending on the tier) instantly after a full charge. The ship slows down during charging, but does not need to stop to jump, and the player can release the activation key before a full charge to travel shorter distances. The light thruster will not work if the destination is inside an object or if the distance traveled is less than 1 km, indicated by the red color on the distance tally instead of blue.

These subsystems can't be manufactured. Tier III lightburners can be bought at faction terminals of the Syndicate with loyalty points if the player has at least tier IV (for lightburner-S) or tier V (for lightburner-M and -L) reputation, while tier X lightburners are ancient.

## Trivia

* Subsystems were previously known as warmods and were only available on warships, with fighter equivalents having a different name.
  + Warmods, once equipped, could not be recovered when players unequipped them.
