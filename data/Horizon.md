Horizon

*“The Horizon is unusually agile for a destroyer. While it sacrifices firepower and durability, it can run laps around other ships in its class.”*

The Horizon is a destroyer-class ship of the Constellation ship line. It is best suited as a cheap but powerful sniper.

## Advantages

The Horizon, like other Constellation-line ships, can warp and move quickly, allowing it to clear swathes of hostile space. Its small turret focus allows it to have more tracking and rate of fire than other destroyers. As expected, the Horizon performs best when away from the front lines. This mitigates its relatively small health pool. A common way to execute this is by outfitting the Horizon with railguns, so that it can still provide adequate fire support while taking little in return. Players should also use the Horizon in high risk scenarios, as it is relatively cheap to replace compared to other destroyers, whilst still being superior to lower-class ships.

The Horizon is the fastest destroyer, as well as the most agile.

## Disadvantages

The Horizon is the weakest destroyer, lacking in both damage and durability. It is outclassed in virtually every role, and cannot be used flexibly. Although it is a shield-focused ship with good deflection, its health is still poor. It has no medium turrets, sacrificing them for more small turret slots. Because of these circumstances, it should not be played like a destroyer. Instead, play it as a frigate or even corvette with the right modules.
