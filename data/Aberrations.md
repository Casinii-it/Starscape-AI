Aberrations

A planet when scanned down.

*This article is about planetary aberrations. Check the Anomalies page for system anomalies.*

Aberration is the name of planetary anomaly-type regions. They don't appear on the system map until the player either goes to the warp hub of a planet or scans a planet with a system probe, releasing orange question marks if an aberration is detected. To lightcruise to the aberration, players must first deploy planetary probes and wait until they finish their survey. Aberrations are identified with a single letter and a random 3-digit number (for example, X-545).

## Aberration Types

### Resource Deposits

Deposits are asteroid fields that can only be found on ringed planets, containing large amounts of a single resource with better ore quality, depending on the system's security class. Depleted deposits become deadspace.

* D-aberrations are usually composed of korrelite, reknite, or gellium; uncommonly of axnit; and rarely of both narcor and red narcor, exclusively in wild space. Individual D-aberrations must be manually checked to determine what kind of ore they contain.
* I-aberrations are composed solely of ice cores, similar to CM anomalies. Heavy water cores can also be found in I-aberrations in wild systems.
* V-aberrations are vexnium fields, very rare and only found in wild systems. They have 1-4 vexnium asteroids and 2-5 ??? guarding them.

### X-Structures

X-aberrations are already destroyed structures that can be found on planets with and without rings, only in systems with PvP enabled, containing varying amounts of secure containers that can be looted. X-aberrations are easily identified if an unscanned aberration is outside a planet's ring (if it has one); if it is inside the ring, there is a good chance it has more secure containers than usual.
