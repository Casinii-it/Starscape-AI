Planets

![Kavanos, Capital of the Mandate, An example of a Gaia Planet](./File:Vanoskavanos.png)

Kavanos, A Gaia Planet

Planets are the permanent explorable locations in the systems currently present in Starscape. Each system has 1 to 12 planets, and some may have more places accessible by lightcruising, including rings and aberrations.

## Warp Hubs and Rings

Warp hubs are the standard location on all planets, being the only place that allows interplanetary and interstellar warp jumps. Rings are unique to ringed planets, possessing only a single planetary asteroid field.

Drones and pirates can appear both in the warp hub and ring.

## Planet Details

Every planet is different, and it's possible to check its characteristics by selecting it in the system map. Some notable details are:

**- Satellites (Moons)** are planet-like bodies that have the same details menu as planets, but instead of the star, their host planet serves as the reference point.

**- World type:** Rocky, Gaia (Earth-like), and Jovian (gas giant), which define its appearance.

**- Distance** defines its distance from the star and, consequently, its number in the planet sequence.

**- Inclination** indicates the orbit's deviation from the system's plane. It may impact its visual position when using probes.

**- Orbital period** shows the time it takes for a body to return to its observed position around the star. The arrangement of the system's bodies is not static and will change with time.

## Trivia

* If the system indicated by an ancient beacon has a ringed planet, there is a chance that a single planet has 6 subregions (1 warp hub, 1 ring, 3 aberrations, and the revealed X-structure).
* All 3 of the warring factions' capitals have a Gaia planet (Foralkus for Hippa and Kavanos for Vanos)
  + Lycentia is notable for having 2 Gaia planets (Lycentia Prime and Tora).
* The 'unknown' planet in G-09 is the only one with an exposed core.
