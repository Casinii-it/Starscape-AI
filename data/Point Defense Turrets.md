Point Defense Turrets

![PDT selection menu.](./File:PDTSelectMenu.png)

*PDT selection menu.*

Point defense turrets, PDTs, or also referred to in-game as P turrets, are fixed hardpoints similar to F turrets. Players can switch them in the ship editing menu by selecting the hardpoint and then the preferred one for a credit fee.

PDTs automatically fire when given line of sight, allowing the user to select up to 4 targets (tractors can track unlimited ore targets). This makes combat PDTs essential for dealing with agile ships, such as interceptors and fighters.

## Hardpoint Information

| Hardpoint | Description | Observations |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Repeater | *"Repeater turrets fire a concentrated burst of energy that is lower damage than other turrets, but more effective against fighters and agile ships due to high turning and fire rates."* | The default P turret is usually the most consistent damage dealer, as it has the best turning speed and second-best base DPS. |
| Pulse | *"Pulse PDTs have lower turning speed and rate of fire than other options, but make up for this by delivering precise beams of energy to their targets."* | Although it is outclassed by the repeater at close ranges, the pulse's high range and hitscan shots make it the best option for kiter ships despite the damage falloff. |
| Chaingun | *"Chaingun turrets are kinetic weapons designed to be most effective at taking out small, fast targets and delivering substantial damage to targeted hulls in close quarters."* | The low turn speed makes the chaingun lose most of its anti-starship niche, but it's quite effective against warships due to the kinetic multiplier and high DPS. |
| Tractor | *"Tractor turrets can be used to retrieve ore which is broken off of asteroids into space."* | Tractors are fundamental for mining because the most effective miner turrets and industrial hardpoints (strip miners and detonators) have low or zero capture rates. |
