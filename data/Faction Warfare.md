Faction Warfare

*This page focuses on the core mechanics of Faction Warfare and offensive gameplay. Check the Warfare Structures page for more information on defensive and industrial gameplay.*

Faction warfare, added in the update of the same name, is one of the main mechanics and gameplay loops in Starscape, consisting of players fighting each other to expand and defend territory on behalf of the NPC faction they have enlisted to, which can be the Kavani Mandate, the Foralkan Empire, or the Lycentian Federation.

Faction warfare takes place primarily in contested systems, situated between the staging areas of the warring factions in the Outer Core. There are 3 frontline sectors:

## System Capture

![](./File:ReinforcementBeaconCapture12.png)

Two player ships near a reinforcement beacon.

### Capture Status

Systems can only be captured if they are directly on the front line—connected by only one warp jump to the opposing faction's territory—and if six hours have passed since they last changed hands. Thus, once a system is taken, it becomes invulnerable for six hours. An additional 40 minutes of invulnerability is added when a neighboring system of an opposing faction is captured, but the system itself remains connected to enemy-controlled systems.

### How to Capture

First, the three generators must be disabled by attacking them (giving 30 minutes to complete the capture before the first generator goes back online). Once all three generators are disabled, the headquarters shield drops, allowing players to attack the Sovereignty Beacon. Destroying the beacon secures the system's capture for the attacking faction and destroys the headquarters.

### Reinforcement Beacons

Reinforcement beacons are large circles in the respective faction's color. They can be secured by attacking forces (remaining in the circle until the bar reaches 100%) to call in allied NPC ships, which will assist in the attack. It is best to do this before attacking the generators, as they distract the generator turrets and enemy military fleets while the players deal most of the damage. Though the aforementioned ships will help with the generators, they will not advance to HQ.

Notice: damaging the hull of a warfare structure or securing a reinforcement beacon will set a global indicator in this system, visible to any player in the game when checking the galactic map.

## Rewards

Capturing systems for your faction grants standing rewards based on the system's defense level: capturing new claims grants a total of 1000 standing, outposts, 2000 standing, garrisons, 3500 standing, and strongholds, 7500 standing. However, the standing reward is divided equally among all players of the invading faction present in the system at the time the Sovereignty Beacon is destroyed, and the total standing reward increases by 10% for each additional player (if 3 players capture an outpost, each player will receive 800 standing).

Veteran and Ace enemy ships will grant credits if destroyed by a player affiliated with an opposing military faction in contested systems. Homeland ships do not grant credits when destroyed.

## Obstacles

### Military Defense Fleets

In the absence of enemy players, faction fleets represent the greatest threat to an invading force. A large fleet is stationed at HQ, and the fleet's power increases with the system's tier and proximity to its staging area. There may also be another large fleet circulating the system, appearing at warp hubs, rings, warfare structures, and reinforcement beacons when a player remains there for too long.

*Main article: Faction Fleets.*

### Secondary Defense Structures

Especially in more heavily defended systems, other obstacles may exist, such as defense platforms that project invulnerability shields over generators and fleet hangars (which continuously generate numerous defense fighters), and faction battleships (built by capital shipyards).

*Main article: Warfare Structures.*

### Planetary Threats

Drones and pirates are a double-edged sword: they can be a nuisance, damaging the player's ship as it travels to points of interest, or serve as a distraction for the system's defenses. They are especially advantageous when there is also a military fleet nearby, allowing players to damage structures while NPCs fight each other.

### Enemy Players

An enemy player joining, especially during a solo-capture attempt, is the primary cause of an incursion's failure. Players allied with the faction controlling the systems you are invading have a significant advantage: they are not targeted by allied structures and ships, meaning you can only retreat if a player is guarding a point of interest. One of the most common (and effective) strategies to deter an invading player is stalling them until the generators are back online. The best way to neutralize enemy players is to call in more players to your side.

Due to warp disruption not being allowed in contested systems, retreating by leaving or warp logging is pretty riskless if the player is not being gunned down while charging.

## User Interface

![](./File:ContestedUserInterface.png)

The system status UI in Istorelt-860.

When a player is in a contested system, a user interface will always appear in the upper left corner of the screen showing the current system status.

There is a bar that indicates approximately how many more supply deliveries are left to improve the system's defense tier. Systems can have 4 different tiers: new claim, outpost, garrison, and stronghold.

Below is the capture status, showing whether this system can be captured, cannot be captured, or how long it will take, so another capture attempt can happen.

The structures section shows the status of existing defense structures and the number of the planet's warp hubs where they are located. Structures are invulnerable until the system becomes capturable. The highlighted structures are the system headquarters (using the HQ icon), the fleet hangar (using the enemy presence icon), and the three generators (using lightning bolt icons).

Light blue means the main structure is invulnerable, requiring the generators to be deactivated in the case of HQ or defense platforms to be destroyed for the rest; dark gray means the fleet hangar was not built yet or was destroyed; light gray means the structure is not protected and can be attacked directly; greyish-red means the generator is deactivated; if the icon is flashing red, it means the structure or its defense platforms are suffering hull damage.

The beacons section shows how many reinforcement beacons are active and the progress towards 100%.

## Strategies

### Solo-Capturing

Solo-capturing entails doing all the work to capture a system, preferably uncontested by enemy players. This rewards massive loyalty point rewards, but it requires speed, luck, and PVE combat experience. Players need a destroyer, with damage output being the most important stat. The Loxodon and the Bulwark are the best options, but the Justice and the Conciliator are also viable if the player wants to draw other players into combat.

First, there is the option to secure the reinforcement beacons. Securing beacons is not required for new claims, but against higher-level systems, they are very useful or even essential; note that securing each beacon can waste enough time to attract enemy players. Second, deactivate the 3 generators as quickly as possible. Third, destroy the HQ fleet while staying out of range of the turrets. Fourth, pray that you manage to destroy the Sovereignty Beacon before 1-an enemy player joins the server, 2-another HQ fleet appears, and 3-the generators come back online.
