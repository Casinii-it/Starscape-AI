Player structures consist of defense and industrial modules that can be deployed by individual players and player factions, lingering indefinitely on the servers until disassembled or destroyed. Player structures can only be built in warp hubs or rings within one of the 8 colonization zones located in wild space.

### Construction

To begin construction, the player must deploy a structure constructor, one for each structure, and select the one they wish to build; the distance must be between 8 and 16 km from the warp-in area. Once the required number of cargo items is provided, construction will begin. Upon completion, the structures will already be supplied with the station fuel and spaceship parts provided for the initial construction.

**- Shield towers** generate a large shield bubble that blocks entry and protects other modules and players within their range (2 km for small towers and 2.5 km for medium towers). Fueling a shield tower with siege fuel will make its shield temporarily invulnerable.

**- Defense platforms** will fire upon any unauthorized ship approaching the station within a 7km radius. These structures are not generated with turrets; they use the same turrets as ships and must be equipped manually (4M 4S for small platforms and 8M 8S for medium platforms).

**- Refineries** process stored ores, including heavy water. They have a passive refining speed of 200% and a yield of 110%, which stacks additively with the player's industrial constellation. A scrapping terminal is not included, and refining does not grant skill points.

**- Cargo depots** function as local storage systems without the restrictions of other modules and can also store cargo. Depots do NOT connect to the CoreSEC Storage Network™; items must eventually be taken to a station for secure storage.

**- Ship refitters** grant access to the ship editing menu and allow players to swap ships. Each change made costs refit points, except for cosmetics. Replacing or removing modifications costs 1 refit point. Ship swapping costs vary with the ship to be equipped.

**- Repair bays** function exactly like those at stations, but require spaceship parts to operate.

### Maintenance

All modules passively consume fuel based on their fuel use stats, while ship refitters and repair bays also consume spaceship parts, but not passively.

### Services

Ship refitters, repair bays, and shield towers can charge a credit fee from other players to be used by them.

## Limitations

* When a module runs out of fuel, it will go offline. Offline modules cannot perform their functions, and their shield and hull regen stats drop to zero; it is possible to remove items from offline modules, but not to store new items. After refueling, the module will go back online after 5 minutes.
* Disassembling the modules only returns half of the construction parts, and the process takes 15 minutes. Note that after disassembly is complete, the construction parts will be dropped as ejected cargo, so do not exit the server during this process to prevent them from despawning.
* Currently, there is no way to locate player structures or deployed constructors other than by seeing them, so always note down their location.
* Each subregion has a complexity limit of 100, limiting how many modules can be placed in a single spacebox.
* Items stored in modules will be dropped if destroyed, as well as metal scraps.
* Ship refitters can store ships, turrets, and modules; refineries can only store ores.
