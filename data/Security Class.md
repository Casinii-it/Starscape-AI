Security Class

The security class of systems governs the loot rarity, danger levels, and what types of player interaction are possible. There are currently five security classes in the game, indicated by the color of the system in the galaxy map.

## Core Systems

Core systems are the least threatening systems in the game, unless you have -1500 standing with the faction to which the system belongs. There are two types of core systems: capitals and staging areas. Capitals are the main hub for that particular faction and where their cloning bays are located. Staging areas are exclusive to the 3 warring factions (the Kavani Mandate, Foralkan Star Empire, and the Lycentian Federation), where players can pick up supplies to haul to system HQs. The capitals of the economic factions (the Syndicate and TU) have both functions.

Little to no worthwhile loot can be acquired in these systems. However, the appeal of systems like the Citadel comes from player interaction, such as trading, the player marketplace, and player faction recruitment.

There are 19 core systems in total.

## Secure Systems

Secure systems are considered the safe area of Starscape, being one of the few places where players cannot threaten each other. Field offices located here give missions that allow new players to gain some starter cash and sharpen their skills; low-tier drones and pirates are present, giving access to easier NPC combat that can serve as a first step for aspiring hunters. Anomalies and Aberrations can also be found in secure space, but are usually not worth pursuing except in the rare event that you find a Wormhole or a Comet anomaly.

There are 263 secure systems in total.

## Contested Systems

Located in the space between the warring factions, contested space is dynamic and can change authority within days. These systems are controlled by at least one of the warring factions and can be captured by enlisted players. Attacking an enemy-controlled system will trigger a global indicator that often will result in more players - allied or not - flocking to that system. For that reason, industrial players should stay clear of vulnerable systems. Various defense structures can be constructed and upgraded if enough supplies are delivered. See Faction Warfare and Warfare Structures for more info.

Even though the loot found within contested space is outclassed by the loot available in wild space, the existence of large dens, fleets, and drone factories does allow for PvE without the risk of losing ships upon dying. X-structures are also present, serving as an alternative to actively mining.

There are 355 contested systems in total.

## Unsecure Systems

Player encounters first become a risk in unsecure systems, allowing unrestricted PvP. Warp Disruptors - particularly, the subsystem variants - can be used in these systems, making it very difficult to escape once engaged. You are most likely to become engaged in PvP combat in the unsecure systems near the Citadel - especially Gatinsir - in choke points before wild space, and those around or on the way to Syndica.

Unsecure systems have identical loot pools to Contested systems.

There are 558 unsecure systems in total.

## Wild Systems

Wild systems, or wild space, make up the vast majority of systems beyond the Core and are divided into 16 major and minor regions. Wild systems have no NPC stations or structures at all, except for frontier outposts and wrecked spice platforms.

PvP is enabled, all subsystems and deployables can be used, and no salvaging is available if players die in these systems. As a player ventures into wild space, they are generally less likely to come across other players. Since this environment primarily encourages industrial and PvE playstyles, most people will be unprepared for an encounter with a player using dedicated PvP loadouts. **If a pirate catches you and you are unprepared to defend yourself, you are very likely to die.**

Wild systems offer the best loot in the game. Exotic resources such as Red Narcor or Vexnium, special satellite X-aberrations, the Drone Battleship, large amounts of pristine-quality ores, and other such rarities can be found here. Systems bordering non-wild systems have NPC threats similar to unsecure and contested space; in the far reaches of wild space, only high-level enemies are present.

## Trivia

* A previously planned system class called "Policed" systems would have PvP enabled, but when players were attacked, police NPCs would spawn. Functioning as an in-between for secure and unsecure systems.
* Uncharted systems were supposedly planned to include “uncharted wormhole systems” in the Starscape Roadmap page, which may refer to wormholes possibly leading to a system that is not part of the map by default.
