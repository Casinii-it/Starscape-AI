Constellation Line

The Constellation line features the fastest warships in the galaxy, but their limited defense, firepower, and set versatility do not encourage direct player combat. They are easily available for newer pilots for their low manufacturing costs.

|  | Starter | Corvette | Frigate | Destroyer |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| T1 | Stratos | Radix | Halo | Horizon |

### Warships

Warships in this line have the best mobility stats available, boosted by their 2 engine rig slots, and a large shield pool, which counters kinetic turrets, making them decent ships for kiting and piracy. However, they are extremely vulnerable to ways of bypassing their speed advantage, such as lightburners, and sustained fire, due to their low total HP.

* Highest speed, warp speed, and second highest agility among warships.
* Second largest shield pool among warships.
* Medium ships only have 3 rig slots (2E, 1R).
* Cheaper to make than other ships of the same class.

### Turret Placement

The Halo and Horizon have their turrets spread across all faces of the ship, leaving no blind spots and allowing for decent broadside firepower. The Radix has the strangest turret placement among combat warships: turrets are concentraded on the top and bottom, but the tilt doesn't allow for a broadside focal point, with 3 turrets being only available diagonally at very long distances.

## Trivia

* The Constellation line ships are named after atmospheric events and the Earth's layers.
* This is the only line to have a starter ship.
* The Constellation and the Mandator lines are the only combat lines to have warships with thrusters seperate from the main body.
* There is an old concept for a Constellation cruiser, called the Meridian
